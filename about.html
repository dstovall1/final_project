About

Devonte Stovall was born and raised in Toledo, Ohio. He began his musical journey as a child, beginning with piano at age 7. Devonte went on to attend Toledo School for the Arts throughout his middle and high school career where he would begin performing professionally under the guidance of his teachers. Devonte would go on to attend Bowling Green State University (BGSU) and would graduate with a Bachelor of Musical Arts, specializing in cello performance and jazz bass studies.


In his youth, Devonte was a member of the ellustrius Sphinx Performance Academy where he would find a home amongst other African American and Latino string players. Devonte was also a member of the Toledo Youth Orchestra and the Toledo International Youth Orchestra where he would have the opportunity to be a cultural abmbasador to the United States while traveling to Japan and Sotuh Korea in the summer of 2011.


In his years at BGSU, Devonte was very active in various music scenes. Devonte was a section cellist of the BGSU Philharmonia as well as being active in BGSU's Lab Band 1 and 2, Vocal Jazz Ensemble, Guitar Ensemble, as well as many other small jazz ensembles. Devonte was also a founding memeber of the traditional celtic music group called Dal Riata (based out of Detroit,MI) as well as the Collingwood Quartet (Toledo,OH). Devonte was also the co-founder of the "BGSU Roots Music Club" and continues to be involved with the organization post-graduation.


Devonte is also a founding member of the bands "The Barn Cats" and "Freight Street". Both bands boast an impressivve discography and have made a name for themselves as regularly performing bands and recording artists. Freight Street released their first self titled album in January of 2020 and despite the hardships of the pandemic, the band recently performed a live stream at Bigfoot Studios (Maumee,OH) and have begun making plans for a tour throughout the midwest, as well as working on a second studio album.
